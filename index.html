Turtle population in Srilanka

Today thier are 250 species of turtles on earth. From all 250 species only seven species live in ocean. Rest of the 243 species live on land or in freshwater pounds. Five out of these seven turtle species in the world visit the shores of Srilankan breed. The 5 species are Green turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley turtle, Loggerhead turtle, and Leatherback turtle.

There are 3 species of fresh water turtles. They are Parker's Black Turtle, Sri Lanka Black Turtle, Flapshell turtle. A normal turtle lays up to 100 eggs and cover it up with sand, after the female turtle will leave to the sea. Thier is a threat for the eggs from dogs and lizards, where they eat the hidden turtle eggs. Also sea birds eat the new born turtles when they try to leave to the sea. So this would affect the population of turtles.

Purpose of turtle hatcheries

Turtle hatcheries are facilities designed place to protect turtle eggs from predators, poarchers. Poarchers of turtle eggs define the people who steal the eggs of turtle for selling purpose. And predators are difined as lizards, snakes, eagles that eat the turtle eggs.The main purpose of turtle hatcheries is to ensure the survival of different species of turtles. These facilities provide a safe and controlled environment for turtle eggs to hatch and release the new born turtles safely to the sea.

How turtle hatcheries work

Turtle hatcheries collect the turtle eggs from thier nests located on beach and take them to a protected area. In turtle hatcheries normaly they build a fenched off sections on the beach as shown in the image to put the eggs to save them from predators. They lables the fenched area with details about the eggs such as the type of turtle, Number of eggs in this fenched section, width and height of egg. This details help for the person who monitors the eggs until they hatch. Once the eggs are hatch the new born turtles ar kept in a safe environment until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean.

Benifits of turtle hatcheries

The major benifit of turtle hatcheries are saving the endangered turtle species around the world. Turtle hatcheries increase the rate of survival of turtles. Also turtle hatcheries are important for humans because they provide opportunities to learn about turtle habitats as well as the important of conservation. Additionally these turtle hatcheries help Srilanka to attract tourists and increase tourism revenue. People around world travel Srilanka to learn more about turtles.
