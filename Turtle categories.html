What are the categories of turrtles in Srilanka?

There are three species of freshwater turtles and one terrestrial tortoise in Sri Lanka. The freshwater turtles are Parker's Black Turtle (Melanochelys trijuga pakeri) Sri Lanka Black Turtle (Melanochelys trijuga thermalis) and the Soft or Flapshell turtle (Lissemys punctata punctata).The terrestrial tortoise is the Star tortoise (Geochelone elegans).

Parker's black turtle

This is a fresh water turtle in South asia and a common seen turtle in srilanka. In srilanka people define this turtle as ගල් ඉබ්බා (Gal Ibba).This is a common species that is widespread in low lands. They are quite common in most still waterbodies and waterways within national parks. They are found even in the city of Colombo and marshes. This turtle is also found in Mannarisland and jaffna penisula and are common in all the dry zones.

Srilanka black turtle

The Indian black turtle (Melanochelys trijuga) is a freshwater turtle in Southern Asia. There are five subspecies, two of which are found in Sri Lanka, M. t. parkeri, found only in Sri Lanka, and M. t. thermalis, found in Sri Lanka, India and the Maldives and known as the Sri Lanka black turtle. This turtle is also found in the kandy lake.

Flapshell turtle

This turtle is found in lowlands but rare in the higher hills. Other than in srilanka this turtle is lso found in India, Nepal, Bangladesh, Myanmar. These turtles used to be extremely common in most water bodies throghout lowlands about 3 decades ago but have become less plentiful in localities outside protected areas due to poaching. This turtle play an important role in keeping waterways clean.

Star tortoise

Star tortoises are found in Sri Lanka. These gorgeous reptiles were aptly named due to the star-shaped pattern on their carapace. They are one of the smallest land tortoises on the planet, reaching only around 35 centimetres (13 inches) in length! Despite their small size and gentle nature, they are able to survive some of the harshest of environments. Star tortoises frequently inhabit dry, arid scrubland and coconut plantations. They are a primarily herbivorous tortoise, dining on grasses, flowers, fresh shoots, berries and fruits when available.Unfortunately, due to illegal poaching and collection for the wildlife trade, the wild population of star tortoises is decreasing.
